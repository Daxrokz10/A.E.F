Albert Einstein, the iconic physicist whose name has become synonymous with genius, revolutionized our understanding of the universe with his groundbreaking theories. Born in Ulm, Germany, in 1879, Einstein developed the theory of relativity, which fundamentally altered our conception of space, time, and gravity. His equation, E=mc², demonstrated the equivalence of mass and energy, laying the groundwork for nuclear energy and fundamentally changing the course of scientific inquiry. Einstein's insights extended beyond physics; he was a fervent advocate for peace, civil rights, and education, leaving a legacy that continues to inspire scientists and thinkers worldwide.
